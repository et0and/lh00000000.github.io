another ridiculously late post. this is documentation of my freestore session that occured on 2019-03-03. this session, i continued my focus on the "bottom shelf".

i carted as much as i could into a classroom so i could sort into piles, such as:

  • metal hardware (later collected into the makita bag)
  • rubber pieces (later put into the center "raw material" drawer)
  • cables and jacks (put into the crates of discarded cable by the wood shop)
  • actuators (put into the electronic components left drawer)
  • visual objects (things like a distinctly recognizaable apple keyboard, a clock, fish bowl, computer mouse, etc)
  • enclosures for metal (i could not see these as likely to be used, so i unfortunately put these metal scraps into technoscrap with the hope that they will be recycled. )
  • electronic boards (put into the right drawer, as a drawer of things that could be desoldered)
  • intact electronic products (put into the top right drawer, as sort of a gadget box)
  • screws went to the shops tray i refer to as the screw graveyard, as well as the nuts/bolts/rods into other similar bins.

meanwhile two students were documenting their floral project in the same room.
